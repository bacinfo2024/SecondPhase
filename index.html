About


Veni, Vidi, Vici     ( أتَيـتُ   فَرَأيـتُ   فَانتَصَـرتُ )
("I came, I saw, I conquered")
is a Latin phrase used to refer to a swift, conclusive victory. The phrase is popularly attributed to Julius Caesar.

**( swift = quick ; conclusive = decisive)**








 

Why Veni, Vidi, Vici ?
 
 

The story behind the quote describes precisely the arabic conquests. Veni vidi vici was wisely considered because the quote is attributed to Julius Caesar who is not an arabic man , however , arabs themselves at that point in history welcomed other cultures. The original quote is :

"فــلا نـامــت أعيــن الجـبنــاء"
